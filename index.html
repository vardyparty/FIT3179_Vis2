Some of the most attended matches feature Melburnian teams, with local rivalries, clashes with history, and big capacity stadiums setting the scene for spectators to descend upon football heartland by the thousands. Annual games such as the ANZAC day match between Collingwood and Essendon, and the Good Friday Superclash between North Melbourne and Carlton - regularly draw in excess of 30 thousand attendees. While Melburnian teams, such as Collingwood, Melbourne, and Richmond tend to draw large crowds, signalled by dark blue gradients, emerging teams such as Gold Coast and Greater Western Sydney struggle to attract many spectators, especially at home. This is indicated by numerous squares with lighter blue gradients.
